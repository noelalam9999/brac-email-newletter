2020 IN RETROSPECT
 
Looking back at 2020, amidst the COVID-19 pandemic BRAC James P Grant School of Public
Health, BRAC University adapted to a nationwide lockdown. Activities and measures were
undertaken and its work on critical health challenges affecting disadvantaged
communities continued.

With initiatives and lessons learnt from the past year, the year 2021 is nationally and
historically of utmost significance. As Bangladesh steps into not only its 50th year of
Independence, but also celebrates a 101 years of the founder of the nation, Bangabandhu
Sheikh Mujibur Rahman, this month; the School continues to etch meaningful impact not
only in the field of public health but also to Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman’s vision
of a great Bangladesh.

BRAC James P Grant School of Public Health, BRAC University, was founded in 2004 in
Dhaka, Bangladesh to address the unmet public health challenges particular to Asia, Africa
and South America. The School ensures premier higher education with unparalleled real life
teaching and learning and as a research institution focuses on critical and emerging
national and global public health challenges.

TRAINING
 
Centre for Professional Skills Development on Public Health (CPSD)
 
The School’s international training hub, Centre for Professional Skills Development on Public Health (CPSD) strengthens and upgrades the requisite professional skills utilized by practitioners, policymakers, development partners, government officials, public advocates, faculty, researchers and frontline health workers.

Approximately 5,000 health and development professionals have received training from the School and in joint collaboration with icddr,b, partner institutions and development partners. JPGSPH extensively and exclusively offers over 50 training, workshops, and seminars annually.

Online Training 2020
 
Since April 2020, BRAC JPGSPH has been hosting a series of webinars every week to address the public
health emergency related to the virus, through sharing of useful resource materials locally and globally
to a wider audience, including BRAC University, health organizations, key stakeholders, partners, and
networks. Faculties and staff from the school are being invited to webinars hosted by national and
international organizations. The School has hosted and participated in more than 37 webinars till date.
